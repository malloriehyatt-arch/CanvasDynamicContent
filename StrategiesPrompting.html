What it does: Reduces vague wording so the AI knows exactly what to do.

Best for: Better first drafts and fewer off-target responses.

Example: “Summarize this article in 3 sentences using simple language for 5th graders.”

Clarity improves output quality

What it does: Shows the AI what a good answer looks like before asking for a new one.

Best for: Tone matching, style imitation, and consistent formatting.

Example: “Here are two strong summaries. Write a third summary in the same style.”

Zero-shot vs. few-shot prompting

What it does: Guides the AI through reasoning one step at a time instead of all at once.

Best for: Problem solving, math, analysis, and multistep tasks.

Example: “List the steps needed to solve 2x + 3 = 11, then solve each step in order.”

Combines chain-of-thought + least-to-most

What it does: Adds useful limits and background so the response is manageable and relevant.

Best for: Grade-level writing, length limits, structured outputs, and reducing overload.

Example: “Create a 5E lesson plan on fractions for 5th grade with objectives, activities, and assessment.”

Combines cognitive load + deliberate constraints

What it does: Treats prompting as a process: ask, review, question, improve, and try again.

Best for: Writing improvement, feedback cycles, and stronger final outputs.

Example: “Write a paragraph, identify one weakness, and revise it with stronger evidence.”
