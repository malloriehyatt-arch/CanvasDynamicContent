Risk Oversight Sorting Activity

Drag each scenario into Low Risk Oversight, Medium Risk Oversight, or High Risk Oversight. As you sort, consider what could go wrong, who could be affected, how easy errors would be to catch, and what level of oversight is appropriate.

Guiding Questions

  • What could go wrong? Who might be affected?
  • How easy would it be to catch and correct problems?
  • Is this mainly formatting, creativity, or brainstorming?
  • Does this require educational judgment or content accuracy?
  • Does this involve student records, evaluation, intervention, or other high-stakes decisions?

Low Risk Oversight

Focus: Administrative tasks, creative brainstorming, and non-sensitive formatting.
  • Mainly format, creativity, or brainstorming?
  • Easy to spot and fix problems?

Medium Risk Oversight

Focus: Content accuracy, instructional materials, and preliminary feedback.
  • Requires educational judgment or content accuracy?
  • Needs review, fact-checking, or adaptation before use?

High Risk Oversight

Focus: PII, high-stakes evaluations, and sensitive interventions.
  • Involves student records, evaluation, or interventions?
  • Could errors or bias have lasting consequences?
