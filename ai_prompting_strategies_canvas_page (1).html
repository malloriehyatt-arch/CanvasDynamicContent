🚀 Effective AI Prompting Strategies

1. RAFT (Role, Audience, Format, Topic)

Structure AI prompts for clarity and specificity.

Example: You are a principal writing a memo to teachers about a new policy in a formal and concise manner.

2. Cognitive Load Theory

Reduce complexity in prompts to maximize efficiency.

Example: Create a 5E lesson plan on fractions for 5th grade, including objectives, activities, and assessments.

3. Chain of Thought (CoT) Prompting

Encourage step-by-step reasoning in AI responses.

Example: Explain how a bill becomes a law in a logical, sequential format.

4. Zero-Shot vs. Few-Shot Prompting

Zero-shot: AI infers without examples.
Few-shot: Provides examples for context.

Examples:
Zero-shot: Summarize this article in three sentences.
Few-shot: Here are two summaries. Now summarize this article in the same style.

5. Persona-Based Prompting

Assign AI a role to refine responses.

Example: Act as a veteran science teacher and explain Newton’s laws to a struggling 9th grader.

6. Socratic Questioning

Use probing questions for deeper responses.

Example: What are the potential downsides of this policy change? What assumptions are being made?

7. Meta-Prompting

Ask AI to generate its own best prompt before answering.

Example: Before answering, create a prompt that would lead to the best response for this question.

8. Deliberate Constraints Prompting

Set limits for length, style, or complexity.

Example: Explain the Pythagorean theorem in exactly two sentences using simple language.

💡 Takeaway: By using these strategies, you can improve the effectiveness of AI responses for educational and administrative tasks.
